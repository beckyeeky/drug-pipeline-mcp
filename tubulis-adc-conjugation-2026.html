1. Executive Summary

Tubulis GmbH is a Munich-based ADC company developing next-generation antibody-drug conjugates using its proprietary P5® conjugation technology. Unlike conventional ADCs that rely on stochastic (random) conjugation or first-generation site-specific approaches, P5® enables highly homogeneous, hydrophilic ADCs with stable linker-payload attachment. This report analyzes the ADC conjugation technology landscape and Tubulis's position within it.

1.1 Key Findings

1. P5® Addresses a Real ADC Industry Problem: Conventional ADCs suffer from heterogeneous DAR (drug-to-antibody ratio), hydrophobic payload-induced aggregation, and premature payload release. P5®'s homogeneous, hydrophilic design directly addresses these limitations — as validated by TUB-040's preclinical data (Mol Cancer Ther 2025, PMID 40667801).
2. Two Assets in FiH, Both RECRUITING: TUB-040 (NaPi2b-targeting, exatecan payload, Ph1/2, n=250) and TUB-030 (undisclosed target, Ph1/2, n=250) are both actively recruiting. This dual-asset strategy de-risks the platform: if one target fails, the P5® technology is validated by the other.
3. NaPi2b is a Clinically Validated Target: NaPi2b was previously targeted by Upifitamab rilsodotin (UpRi, Mersana/Ph3, terminated for futility). TUB-040's differentiation — stable linker, exatecan payload (topo I inhibitor, not auristatin) — may overcome the limitations that led to UpRi's failure.
4. ADC Conjugation Competition is Intense: Established platforms (Seagen's maleimide conjugation, Synaffix' GlycoConnect, ImmunoGen's DGN549) compete for BD partnerships. Emerging technologies (P5®, Oxford BioTherapeutics' platform) are vying for differentiation. P5®'s dual advantage (homogeneity + hydrophilicity) is a defensible position.
5. Key Readout: 2027-2028: Both TUB-040 (NAPISTAR 1-01) and TUB-030 have primary completion dates in 2027-2028. The first clinical data will be critical for platform validation.

3.2 NaPi2b Target Context

NaPi2b (sodium-dependent phosphate transport protein 2b) is a cell surface receptor overexpressed in ovarian cancer (~70-90%) and lung adenocarcinoma (~60-80%). It was previously targeted by:

  • Upifitamab rilsodotin (UpRi, Mersana) — NaPi2b ADC with auristatin payload. UPGRADE-A Ph3 (ovarian cancer) terminated due to futility in 2023. Safety concerns included ocular toxicity and thrombocytopenia.
  • XMT-1592 (Mersana) — Second-generation NaPi2b ADC with DOLA payload. Discontinued after UpRi failure.

TUB-040 differentiates through its exatecan payload (topoisomerase I inhibitor, same class as Enhertu's DXd) and P5® conjugation — potentially avoiding the ocular toxicity and narrow TI that affected UpRi.

4.2 Strategic Recommendations

1. First Clinical Data is Everything. As a private platform company, TUB-040's first clinical data will determine Tubulis's valuation and BD partnering potential. Every resource should be focused on delivering clean safety + preliminary efficacy data from NAPISTAR 1-01.
2. NaPi2b Requires Cautious Optimism. The target is validated (high expression, known biology) but the precedent of UpRi's failure means investors and partners will scrutinize TUB-040's therapeutic index carefully. Data showing a wider TI than UpRi would be highly differentiating.
3. Platform Story Needs Clinical Proof. P5® technology is scientifically compelling, but the ADC landscape is littered with promising platforms that failed to translate. TUB-030's undisclosed target provides a second shot at platform validation — its selection is a critical strategic decision.
4. BD Partnering Window is Now. Large pharma is actively seeking next-generation ADC platforms (Pfizer/Seagen, AbbVie/ImmunoGen, Merck/Kelun). Tubulis should proactively engage potential partners while the P5® story is still early enough to command platform-level premiums.

5. Key Takeaways for Dr. Dominik Schumacher

1. Platform First, Assets Second. P5® is Tubulis's core value driver. Every clinical data point should be framed as platform validation, not just asset-level progress.
2. NaPi2b is a High-Risk, High-Reward Target. The target is well-validated but carries the UpRi overhang. TUB-040's exatecan payload and P5® stability are credible differentiators — but convincing the market will require head-to-head context.
3. Two Assets Halve the Risk. Having TUB-030 with an undisclosed target in parallel FiH trials is strategically sound. Even if one program hits a toxicity issue, the other can carry the platform story forward.
4. The ADC Gold Rush Creates a Partnering Window. Current BD appetite for ADC platforms is at an all-time high (Pfizer/Seagen $43B, AbbVie/ImmunoGen $10B, Merck/Kelun $13B). Tubulis should capitalize on this window with a proactive partnering strategy.
5. Cash Runway Requires Discipline. With Series B closed in 2023 and two Ph1/2 trials running, cash management is critical. The €55M from 2023 is likely 3-4 years of runway — leaving 1-2 years before Series C or BD partnership needs to close.
