Competitive Landscape: AML

17 EU-approved therapies in AML (including Mylotarg, Rydapt, Xospata, Tibsovo, Vanflyta, Vyxeos) — but no approved multispecific T-cell engager.

MP0533 occupies a unique position: it is the first trispecific T-cell engager in clinical trials for AML, simultaneously addressing CD33, CD123, and CD70. The nearest competitors are bispecific (CD33×CD3 from Amgen, CD123×CD3 from MacroGenics/StemLine). Critically, Amgen's CD33×CD3 BiTE AMG 330 was terminated (NCT02520427, Amgen portfolio restructuring, completed Jan 2022) — the competitive landscape is thinner than commonly assumed. MP0533's triple targeting directly addresses antigen escape — the most common resistance mechanism in bispecific T-cell engagers. Preclinical data published in Cancer Immunology Research (2024, PMID: 38683145) demonstrated superior T-cell activation and leukemic stem cell killing vs. bispecific combinations.

3 Key Findings for Dr. Amstutz

Finding 1: Trispecific advantage in a bispecific field
MP0533 is the first and only trispecific T-cell engager in clinical testing for AML (CD33×CD123×CD70). All competitors (Amgen, MacroGenics, StemLine) pursue bispecific approaches. Triple targeting reduces antigen escape — a documented liability for CD33-monotargeting (Mylotarg) and bispecifics. The preclinical publication in Cancer Immunology Research (2024) shows superior in vitro T-cell activation and leukemic stem cell killing vs. bispecific combinations.
Finding 2: Combo strategy with azacitidine/venetoclax opens first-line market access
The Phase 2 extension of NCT05673057 includes combination arms with azacitidine + venetoclax — the current standard of care for unfit AML patients. This positions MP0533 not as a "last resort" monotherapy but early in the treatment cascade. If MP0533 demonstrates an acceptable safety profile in combination (acknowledging that trispecific T-cell engagers carry theoretically higher CRS risk), the addressable market expands significantly beyond R/R monotherapy.
Finding 3: The AML T-cell engager window is narrowing — differentiation timing matters
The competitive landscape is thinner than often assumed. Amgen terminated its CD33×CD3 BiTE AMG 330 (NCT02520427, portfolio restructuring, Jan 2022) and AMG 673 was also removed from the pipeline. Remaining competitors: MacroGenics' CD123×CD3 DART (flotetuzumab) has Phase 2 data but uncertain development path; StemLine's vibecotamab is in early Phase 1. This means MP0533 faces less immediate competition than a typical AML landscape would suggest — but the trispecific complexity story must be clearly communicated to investors and potential partners. The competitor that matters most may not be another T-cell engager, but established AML standards (azacitidine/venetoclax, FLT3 inhibitors, IDH inhibitors) that define the current treatment paradigm.
