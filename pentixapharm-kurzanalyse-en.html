Three Key Findings

1. Unoccupied Orphan Space in CNS Lymphoma

No EMA- or FDA-approved therapy exists specifically for PCNSL. Standard of care (HD-MTX-based regimens) is off-label and associated with neurotoxicity. PentixaTher addresses a high-unmet-need orphan space with first-in-class CXCR4 endoradiotherapy. Blood-brain barrier penetration of the PentixaTher ligand has been documented in preclinical and clinical imaging studies — a key advantage over antibody-based approaches in CNS indications.

2. Theranostic Platform = Pipeline-in-a-Pipeline

The CXCR4 ligand scaffold is identical for diagnosis (⁶⁸Ga) and therapy (⁹⁰Y/¹⁷⁷Lu). Every new indication where [⁶⁸Ga]Ga-PentixaFor detects CXCR4 overexpression opens a therapy pathway for PentixaTher. Current: CNS Lymphoma, AML, Multiple Myeloma, Lymphoma, Hypertension — five indications from one scaffold. This is a fundamentally different investment profile than single-asset biotechs.

3. Phase 3 Diagnostic De-Risks Therapeutic Pipeline

The PANDA Phase 3 study start in hypertension (IND submitted May 2026) is not just a standalone value driver — it validates the regulatory platform with both FDA and EMA. A successful Phase 3 for the diagnostic asset would de-risk the entire CXCR4 pipeline and establish Pentixapharm as a credible radiopharmaceutical developer. Additionally, the diagnostic creates a commercial foothold in clinical practice that prepares the therapeutic rollout.

One Watchpoint

Recruitment — CNS Lymphoma: Trial NCT06132737 (target: 15 patients) has been recruiting since November 2023 (~2.5 years). For a Best-of-5 dose escalation in an orphan CNS indication (incidence ~0.5/100,000), this timeline is consistent with realistic enrollment expectations. The achievement of Dose Level 4 in the parallel AML trial suggests no systemic recruitment issues across the platform.
