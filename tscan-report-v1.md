## 1. Executive Summary

TScan Therapeutics ($69M market cap) is a clinical-stage TCR-T company developing a uniquely multiplexed approach to T cell receptor-engineered cell therapy. The company's dual strategy — hematologic malignancies (TSC-100/101) and solid tumors (TSC-200 series T-Plex) — positions it in the fastest-growing segment of adoptive cell therapy.

**Key Findings:**

1. **T-Plex multiplexing is a genuine differentiator:** No competitor is testing up to 27 different TCR-T combinations in a single basket trial (NCT05973487). This combinatorial approach addresses antigen escape — the primary failure mode of single-target TCR-T in solid tumors.

2. **TSC-100/101 targets an unmet medical need:** Post-HCT relapse in AML/MDS has NO approved targeted therapies. The HA-1/HA-2 minor histocompatibility antigens are well-validated targets with published proof-of-concept (PMID 38683966: HA-1 TCR-T in relapsed leukemia).

3. **Cash runway concern:** With $69M MCap and negative operating margins (-2,967%), the company likely faces financing needs within 12-18 months. The Phase 1 data readout (mid-2026) is a binary catalyst — positive data opens financing, negative data threatens survival.

4. **Competitive position:** TScan sits between Immunocore ($1.47B, approved product) and distressed peers (Alaunos ~$6M). Unlike Adaptimmune (near-delisting) and Alaunos (cash crisis), TScan maintains active recruitment across both trials.

5. **Most similar to Immatics ($1.6B) in platform breadth** but at 1/23rd the valuation — this discount reflects clinical immaturity (Phase 1 vs Phase 3) rather than platform quality.

> **What this means for TScan:** The $69M valuation vs Immatics' $1.6B creates a 23x gap that narrows with each data point. The company's differentiation lies NOT in individual TCR potency but in the T-Plex combinatorial concept — a message that needs stronger articulation in investor communications.

## 3. TScan Platform & Pipeline

### 3.1 Platform Overview

TScan's platform comprises three integrated technologies:

1. **TScan-I (Class I Discovery):** Genome-scale platform for identifying CD8+ T cell epitopes presented on MHC Class I. Published in *Nature Biotechnology* (PMID 38956325). Targets tumor-associated antigens (TAA) including PRAME, MAGE-A1, MAGE-A4, MAGE-C2.

2. **TScan-II (Class II Discovery):** Genome-scale platform for CD4+ T cell epitope discovery on MHC Class II. Published in *Cell* (PMID 38016469). Opens the door to CD4+ helper T cell therapies — a nascent but high-potential space.

3. **T-Plex (Multiplexing):** Proprietary approach to administering multiple TCR-T products simultaneously or sequentially to address antigen heterogeneity and escape. This is TScan's core differentiation.

### 3.3 T-Plex: The Differentiation Story

The TSCAN-002 basket trial (NCT05973487) is unique in the TCR-T space:

- **840 patients** enrolled across multiple tumor types (H&N, NSCLC, melanoma, cervical, ovarian, anal)
- **27 distinct intervention arms** — monotherapies AND dual combinations
- **Repeat dosing** — patients can receive multiple TCR-T infusions targeting different antigens
- **4 HLA restrictions** — A*02:01, A*01:01, B*07:02, C*07:02

> **What this means for TScan:** No competitor is testing combinatorial TCR-T at this scale. If even 2-3 T-Plex combinations show superior durability vs monotherapy, this becomes a platform-level value proposition that justifies partnership/licensing interest from larger players.

## 4. Lead Candidate Deep Dive: TSC-100 / TSC-101

### 4.1 Target Biology

| Target | Expression | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **HA-1** (TSC-100) | Hematopoietic cells (minor H antigen) | MRD marker after HCT; immunogenic target validated in GVHD |
| **HA-2** (TSC-101) | Hematopoietic cells (minor H antigen) | Similar to HA-1; combined targeting reduces relapse |

Minor histocompatibility antigens (HA-1, HA-2) are immunogenic peptides presented on HLA molecules that differ between donor and recipient. They are:
- Highly expressed on hematopoietic cells (including leukemic blasts)
- Absent from GVHD target tissues (skin, liver, GI tract)
- Clinically validated: HA-1-specific T cells associated with GVL effect without GVHD

### 4.2 Trial Design (NCT05473910)

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Design** | Multi-center, non-randomized, concurrent controlled, multi-arm, Phase 1 |
| **Population** | AML, MDS, or ALL patients post-allogeneic HCT |
| **Intervention** | SOC + TSC-100 or SOC + TSC-101 vs SOC alone (concurrent control) |
| **Enrollment** | 75 patients (target) |
| **Primary Endpoint** | DLTs, AEs (safety) |
| **Secondary Endpoints** | DFS at 6/12/18/24 months, relapse rates, OS, immunogenicity |
| **Sites** | 15 US academic centers (City of Hope, MD Anderson, MSK, Dana-Farber, etc.) |
| **Started** | November 2022 |
| **Primary Completion** | June 2026 (any day now!) |
| **Published Precedent** | HA-1 TCR-T: 4/6 CRs in relapsed leukemia post-HCT (PMID 38683966, *Blood* 2024) |

### 4.3 Key Risks

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| Small sample size for efficacy readout (n≈30-40 evaluable) | Medium | Controlled arm provides internal benchmark |
| HA-1-negative relapse (antigen escape) | Low | Concurrent TSC-100 + TSC-101 covers both targets |
| Manufacturing complexity (donor-derived) | Medium | Centralized manufacturing at Waltham facility |

> **What this means for TScan:** The June 2026 primary completion is a critical catalyst. Published data from HA-1 TCR-T (PMID 38683966) showed 4/6 responses in relapsed leukemia — if TScan matches this, TSC-100/101 becomes a registration-enabling candidate. If not, it's a platform proof-of-concept failure.

## 5. Lead Candidate Deep Dive: TSC-200 Series (T-Plex)

### 5.1 Program Architecture

The T-Plex platform covers 5 antigen targets across 4 HLA restrictions, enabling combinatorial approaches unmatched in the field:

| TCR | Target | HLA Restriction | Tumor Coverage |
|-----|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| TSC-200 | PRAME | A*02:01 | NSCLC, melanoma, sarcoma, ovarian |
| TSC-201 | MAGE-A1 | B*07:02 | H&N, NSCLC, bladder |
| TSC-202 | MAGE-A4 | A*02:01 | Esophageal, NSCLC, ovarian |
| TSC-203 | MAGE-C2 | A*02:01 | Melanoma, NSCLC, H&N |
| TSC-204 | HPV16 | A*02:01 / C*07:02 | HPV+ cancers (cervical, H&N, anal) |

### 5.2 Trial Design (NCT05973487)

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Design** | Multi-center, non-randomized, multi-arm, open-label, Phase 1 basket |
| **Population** | Locally advanced (unresectable) or metastatic solid tumors |
| **Interventions** | 27 arms: 5 monotherapies + 22 dual combinations |
| **Enrollment** | 840 patients |
| **Primary Endpoint** | Safety, RP2D determination |
| **Secondary Endpoints** | ORR, DoR, PFS, feasibility of repeat dosing |
| **Key Eligibility** | HLA-A*02:01, A*01:01, B*07:02, or C*07:02 + tumor expressing PRAME, MAGE-A1/4/C2, or HPV16 |
| **Sites** | 20 US academic centers |
| **Started** | May 2024 |
| **Primary Completion** | December 2026 |
| **Patients Screened via** | TSCAN-003 (NCT05812027) — dedicated screening study |

### 5.3 Unique Aspects

- **Repeat dosing:** TCR-T can be administered multiple times (unlike CAR-T which is typically single-dose)
- **Sequential targeting:** Patient can receive PRAME-targeted TSC-200, then upon progression, switch to HPV16-targeted TSC-204
- **Combination:** Simultaneous dual targeting to prevent antigen escape — the primary failure mechanism of single-target ACT

> **What this means for TScan:** The T-Plex concept addresses the central challenge of ACT in solid tumors: antigen heterogeneity. If the Phase 1 data show that T-Plex combinations outperform monotherapy (even marginally), TScan has a platform story that Immunocore (single-target tebentafusp) and Adaptimmune (mostly single-target) cannot tell.

## 11. Strategic Positioning

### 11.1 SWOT Analysis

**Strengths:**
- T-Plex multiplexing is unique in TCR-T space
- Strong scientific leadership (MacBeath, Harvard/HHMI)
- Dual hem/solid tumor pipeline de-risks platform
- HA-1/HA-2 targets are biologically validated (PMID 38683966)
- Low valuation creates asymmetric return potential

**Weaknesses:**
- Pre-revenue with limited cash runway (~7-9 months)
- All programs in Phase 1 — no approved product
- Manufacturing complexity (autologous, donor-derived)
- HLA restriction limits addressable patient population
- Stock trading below book value signals market skepticism

**Opportunities:**
- T-Plex combination data could redefine TCR-T treatment paradigm
- Partnership with larger pharma (Amgen collaboration for autoimmune targets exists)
- Expansion beyond oncology (autoimmune TCR-T via TScan-II platform)
- PRAME validation by Immunocore's Phase 3 benefits TScan's TSC-200

**Threats:**
- Immunocore's brenetafusp (Phase 3 PRAME-targeted) sets competitive benchmark
- Cash depletion before meaningful data readout
- Adaptimmune-like clinical failure would depress entire TCR-T sector
- Regulatory uncertainty for multiplexed cell therapy products

### 11.2 Strategic Recommendations

**For TScan Management:**

1. **Accelerate T-Plex data disclosure at medical meetings** — Even small cohort data (n=5-10) at ASCO 2026 or SITC 2026 would significantly de-risk the platform narrative for investors.

2. **Strengthen the multiplexing message** — Current investor materials underemphasize the 27-arm combinatorial design. This is TScan's ONLY defensible competitive advantage vs Immunocore/Immatics.

3. **Explore non-dilutive financing** — The $6.00 analyst target suggests institutional interest if Phase 1 data is clean. Consider ATM offering post-data-readout rather than pre-data bridge financing.

4. **Leverage Amgen collaboration narrative** — The autoimmune TCR discovery partnership (Crohn's disease) provides TScan-II platform validation and potential revenue milestone. Promote this alongside oncology pipeline.

5. **Prepare for registration pathway discussions** — If TSC-100/101 Phase 1 shows compelling DFS benefit vs concurrent control, request End-of-Phase 1 meeting with FDA for accelerated development path.

**For Investors/Partners:**

1. TScan represents a **high-risk, high-reward asymmetric bet** — $69M MCap vs potential platform value of $500M+ with positive Phase 1 data
2. The **T-Plex concept is unproven but unduplicable** — if it works, TScan owns multiplexed TCR-T
3. **Primary risk is financing, not science** — the company needs data before cash runs out
4. **Most comparable to early Immatics** (pre-Phase 3, ~$200M MCap) — with T-Plex differentiation
