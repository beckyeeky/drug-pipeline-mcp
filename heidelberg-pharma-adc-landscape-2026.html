1   Executive Summary

KEY FINDING The multiple myeloma ADC space remains distinctly nascent. Despite the approval of seven BCMA-targeting therapies across three modalities (CAR-T, bispecifics, ADCs), only one ADC — Blenrep (belantamab mafodotin, GSK) — is currently authorised in the EU. Blenrep's re-approval in combination regimens (DREAMM-7, DREAMM-8) has validated the ADC modality in MM, but its significant ocular toxicity (keratopathy in ~70% of patients) creates a clear competitive opening for a differentiated ADC with a superior safety profile.

The multiple myeloma treatment landscape has undergone a fundamental transformation over the past decade. The field has evolved from a reliance on immunomodulatory drugs (IMiDs) and proteasome inhibitors (PIs) to a richly diversified armamentarium that includes CD38-targeting monoclonal antibodies, CAR-T cell therapies, bispecific T-cell engagers, and — most recently — antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs). Despite these advances, multiple myeloma remains an incurable disease for the vast majority of patients, with virtually all patients eventually relapsing or becoming refractory to available therapies.

Heidelberg Pharma AG (FSE: HPHA, market cap ~€113M) occupies a unique and potentially disruptive position within this landscape. The company's proprietary ATAC (Amanitin) platform leverages α-amanitin — a toxin derived from the green death cap mushroom (Amanita phalloides) — as a payload with a fundamentally different mechanism of action from any other ADC in clinical development. Amanitin inhibits RNA polymerase II, inducing apoptosis even in quiescent and dormant cancer cells. This is a critical differentiator in multiple myeloma, where dormant cell populations drive eventual relapse.

2.2 ATAC Technology Platform

The ATAC (Amanitin-Targeted Antibody Conjugate) platform is Heidelberg Pharma's core asset and primary differentiator. The platform uses α-amanitin, a bicyclic octapeptide toxin from the death cap mushroom Amanita phalloides, as its warhead payload. Unlike conventional ADC payloads (auristatins, maytansinoids, PBD dimers) that target microtubules or DNA, α-amanitin is a highly selective and potent inhibitor of RNA polymerase II.

Key mechanistic advantages of the ATAC platform:

  • Kills quiescent cells: Unlike microtubule inhibitors that only target dividing cells, RNA pol II inhibition works in both proliferating and dormant cancer cells — critical for eliminating minimal residual disease in MM.
  • No cross-resistance: Amanitin's MOA is entirely orthogonal to standard-of-care MM agents (IMiDs, PIs, CD38 mAbs) and to other ADC payloads (MMAF, DM1, PBD). HDP-101 has demonstrated activity in belantamab-refractory preclinical models.
  • Favorable bystander effect: The amanitin payload shows a balanced bystander killing profile, enabling activity against antigen-heterogeneous tumour populations.
  • Stable linker technology: The proprietary linker design ensures plasma stability while enabling efficient payload release in the tumour microenvironment.

3.2 Mechanism of Action

MECHANISTIC DIFFERENTIATION HDP-101 delivers α-amanitin selectively to BCMA-expressing myeloma cells. Upon internalisation, the amanitin payload is released and binds to RNA polymerase II with picomolar affinity, inhibiting transcription. This triggers protein synthesis arrest and apoptosis through a mechanism entirely distinct from all currently approved MM therapies, including Blenrep (MMAF microtubule inhibitor), CAR-T cells, and bispecific T-cell engagers.

The HDP-101 published literature (Strassz et al., Blood 2020; Mol Cancer Ther 2021) demonstrates that HDP-101 induces cell death in both proliferating and resting multiple myeloma cells — a property unique among BCMA-targeted agents. This is particularly relevant for the del(17p) high-risk patient population, where preclinical data (preprint, Research Square 2024) showed HDP-101 superior to belantamab mafodotin.

4   Multiple Myeloma Treatment Landscape

4.1 Disease Overview

Multiple myeloma (MM) is a haematological malignancy characterised by clonal proliferation of plasma cells in the bone marrow. It accounts for approximately 10% of all haematologic malignancies and ~2% of all cancers. Global incidence is estimated at ~180,000 new cases annually, with prevalence growing due to an ageing population and improving survival (median overall survival now 7–10 years in the novel agent era).

5.2 Blenrep: The Incumbent — Successes and Vulnerabilities

Blenrep (belantamab mafodotin) is the only approved ADC in multiple myeloma and therefore the direct comparator for HDP-101. Understanding Blenrep's clinical and commercial trajectory is essential to positioning HDP-101.

Clinical trajectory:

  • DREAMM-2 (monotherapy): Accelerated FDA approval (Aug 2020) based on 32% ORR in heavily pre-treated patients. Withdrawn from US market Nov 2022 after DREAMM-3 confirmatory trial failed to meet PFS endpoint.
  • DREAMM-7 (BVd vs DVd, 2L+): Phase 3 victory — BVd showed significant PFS benefit vs daratumumab + Vd (HR 0.41, p<0.001). Updated OS analysis confirmed overall survival benefit (Lancet Oncol 2025).
  • DREAMM-8 (BPd vs PVd, 2L+): Phase 3 success — BPd showed significant PFS benefit vs bortezomib + Pd in lenalidomide-exposed patients (Lancet Haematol 2025).
  • EU re-authorisation: Blenrep is now EU-authorised for 2L+ in two combination regimens: BVd and BPd.
BLENREP'S ACHILLES HEEL Ocular toxicity is the defining limitation of belantamab mafodotin. FAERS data shows 2,809 total adverse event reports, with keratopathy (839 reports), visual acuity reduced (766), dry eye (389), night blindness (317), and photophobia (191) dominating the profile. Microcystic keratopathy requiring dose holds and ophthalmologic monitoring affects ~70% of patients. This creates a clear and exploitable competitive opening for a BCMA ADC without ocular toxicity.

6   Cross-Modality Comparison: BCMA Targeting

6.1 The BCMA Targeting Landscape

B-cell maturation antigen (BCMA) has emerged as the most clinically validated target in multiple myeloma, with seven EU-approved therapies and multiple additional candidates in development. The BCMA targeting space is uniquely diverse, spanning four distinct therapeutic modalities:

BCMA CAR-T

Abecma (BMS), Carvykti (JNJ/Legend)

Deep, durable responses. 2L+ approval (Carvykti). Manufacturing delay, lymphodepletion toxicity, CRS/ICANS, high cost (~€400k+).

BCMA Bispecifics

Tecvayli (JNJ), Elrexfio (Pfizer), Lynozyfic (Regeneron)

Off-the-shelf, active post-CAR-T. CRS is common (50–70%), frequent dosing (weekly/fortnightly). Talent: Talvey targets GPRC5D, not BCMA.

BCMA ADC

Blenrep (GSK), HDP-101 (Heidelberg)

Off-the-shelf, no CRS, convenient dosing (every 3 weeks). Blenrep limited by ocular toxicity. HDP-101 aims to improve on this profile.

STRATEGIC INSIGHT ADCs occupy the "safest" end of the BCMA targeting spectrum. CAR-T delivers the deepest responses but with logistical complexity and significant toxicity. Bispecifics offer convenience but carry CRS risk. ADCs (particularly those with well-tolerated payloads like HDP-101's amanitin) can serve as combination backbones earlier in the treatment pathway — a role neither CAR-T nor bispecifics can easily fill due to overlapping toxicities.

6.3 The Emerging Sequencing Paradigm

The optimal sequence of BCMA-targeted therapies is one of the most debated questions in modern myeloma management. Key considerations:

  • CAR-T first? Carvykti is now approved 2L+ — earlier CAR-T may capture patients before T-cell fitness deteriorates. But manufacturing delays risk progression, and lymphodepletion is taxing.
  • Bispecifics first? Off-the-shelf availability and activity post-CAR-T make bispecifics an attractive salvage option. But the CRS burden limits use in frail patients.
  • ADC first? Blenrep's re-approval in 2L+ validates ADC + backbone combination (BVd, BPd). The favourable safety profile (no CRS, no lymphodepletion) makes this the most broadly applicable approach, particularly for older/frailer patients who constitute the majority of the MM population.

7.3 The ADC Market Opportunity in MM

We estimate the addressable ADC market in MM at $2–4 billion annually by 2030, contingent on: (1) successful combination regimen development; (2) expansion to earlier lines of therapy; and (3) differentiation from CAR-T and bispecific modalities. Key assumptions:

  • Blenrep (assuming successful re-uptake) could achieve $500M–1B in peak sales in the EU/UK/joint markets with combo regimens.

8   Strategic Positioning for Heidelberg Pharma

8.1 Current Position Assessment

Heidelberg Pharma is a micro-cap biotech (market cap ~€113M) with a single clinical-stage asset (HDP-101) that has the potential to redefine the BCMA ADC category, but faces substantial execution risk. The company's market cap implies limited credit for the ATAC platform outside HDP-101, and limited financial flexibility to fund multiple late-stage programs simultaneously.

SWOT ANALYSIS Strengths: Novel MOA (RNA pol II inhibition) with clear differentiation vs all BCMA competitors. Robust preclinical data showing activity in dormancy models and del(17p). No ocular toxicity expected. Established manufacturing and clinical supply chain. Partnered China strategy (Huadong).

Weaknesses: Phase 1/2 stage — no clinical efficacy data yet. Single clinical-stage asset. Limited financial resources (~€113M market cap). Prior pipeline (uPA, girentuximab) did not reach commercialisation.

Opportunities: Frail/elderly MM population (50%+ of patients) underserved by CAR-T/bispecifics. Combination backbone potential. China market via Huadong. Platform expansion to CD37 (NHL) and PSMA (prostate).

Threats: Blenrep re-establishing ADC standard. CAR-T moving to 1L (CARTITUDE-5, -6). Bispecific competition intensifying. Potential new ADC competitors.

8.2 Strategic Recommendation 1: Own the Frail/Elderly Position

Thesis: CAR-T and bispecifics have significant toxicity (CRS, ICANS, lymphodepletion, cytopenias) that limits their use in the ~50% of MM patients aged ≥70 or those with comorbidities. HDP-101's expected safety profile (no CRS, no lymphodepletion, no keratopathy) positions it as the ideal BCMA-targeted therapy for the underserved frail/elderly population.

Execution: Design a registrational trial specifically in patients ≥70 or with ECOG PS 2 who are "CAR-T/bispecific-ineligible." Primary endpoint: ORR + safety composite. Secondary: geriatric assessment endpoints. This would create a differentiated label even before broader population data.

8.3 Strategic Recommendation 2: Build the Combination Database

Thesis: Blenrep's comeback was built on combination data (DREAMM-7, DREAMM-8). HDP-101's path to registration almost certainly requires a combination strategy. The ideal partners are pomalidomide+dex (Blenrep's BPd regimen validates this IMiD backbone) and carfilzomib+dex (synergistic with RNA pol II inhibition).

Execution: Initiate a Phase 1b/2 combination trial of HDP-101 + pomalidomide + dexamethasone (HPd regimen) in 2L+ MM. This would be directly comparable to Blenrep's DREAMM-8 data. Secondary: explore HDP-101 + carfilzomib + dex (HKd regimen) for PI-based combination.

8.4 Strategic Recommendation 3: Leverage China for First Approval

Thesis: The Huadong partnership and active China trial (NCT07529782, initiated March 2026, 5 sites) create a potential first-approval pathway in China. China's NMPA has demonstrated willingness to approve innovative therapies with novel mechanisms of action, particularly for orphan/rare disease indications. A China-first approval would provide: (1) early revenue; (2) clinical data for global registration; (3) proof-of-concept for the ATAC platform.

Execution: Prioritise China enrolment. The Phase 1 China trial (n=15) could rapidly generate safety data. A Phase 2 China expansion (n=40–60) could support an NMPA NDA by H1 2027. Use China data to support FDA/EMA registration via cross-reference.

8.5 Strategic Recommendation 4: Pursue PRIME and Breakthrough Designations

Thesis: Amanitin's novel MOA (first-in-class RNA pol II inhibitor ADC) qualifies HDP-101 for expedited regulatory pathways. EMA PRIME designation and FDA Breakthrough Therapy designation would significantly accelerate development and increase company valuation.

Execution: Submit EMA PRIME application based on: (1) novel MOA addressing unmet need; (2) preclinical superiority to standard of care; (3) favourable early safety profile. FDA Breakthrough application based on preliminary clinical evidence of improvement over existing therapy. Both designations are feasible given the mechanistic differentiation.

8.6 Strategic Recommendation 5: De-Risk Through Pipeline Expansion

Thesis: A single-asset biotech is inherently fragile. While HDP-101 is the value driver, advancing HDP-102 (CD37-ATAC, NHL) and HDP-103 (PSMA-ATAC, prostate) to IND/Phase 1 over the next 12–18 months would: (1) validate the ATAC platform as a modular technology; (2) diversify risk; (3) increase partnership/out-licensing optionality.

Execution: Allocate 15–20% of R&D budget to HDP-102 IND filing (CD37 has strong preclinical rationale in NHL, a large and less crowded ADC target). Consider out-licensing HDP-103 (PSMA) to a larger partner with prostate cancer commercial infrastructure to generate non-dilutive capital for HDP-101 development.

9.3 AMNOG / HTA Assessment (Germany)

As a German-headquartered company, the home market is strategically important. Under AMNOG rules:

  • Orphan drugs (EU Orphan Designation) benefit from free pricing for the first year post-approval, followed by a negotiated benefit assessment based on added benefit vs comparator.
  • The small patient population for late-line RRMM (~2,000–3,000 patients in Germany) limits revenue ceiling unless expanded to earlier lines.

9.4 China Regulatory Pathway (NRDL)

The China market via Huadong partnership is a key strategic asset:

  • NRDL (National Reimbursement Drug List) inclusion would require significant price discounts (typically 40–70%) but would provide access to China's large MM patient population (~20,000 new cases/year, growing rapidly).
  • China-first approval strategy would make HDP-101 one of the first China-developed ADCs with a novel payload — a significant reputational and commercial asset for both Heidelberg and Huadong.
